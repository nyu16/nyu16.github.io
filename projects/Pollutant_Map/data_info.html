Data Information Page

Sources:

All data used in this map was gathered from the Washington Geospatial Open Data Portal. In total, six separate datasets were gathered, with one pollutant variable in the map being derived from each individual dataset. Shown in the table below are the datasets, the used variables, and the sources of the datasets.

Variable Description:

Count_

The three-year mean concentration of annual particulate matter particles with diameters 2.5 micrometers and smaller for 2009-2011. (More information)

Percent_Units_with_Lead

The number and percent of housing units built before 1980, including single homes and multiple residence units such as apartments. (More information)

Proximity_to_Heavy_Traffic_Roadways

Present estimates of the number of people exposed to air pollutants from living near busy roadways. (More information)

Avg_Ozone_Conc_ppb_km2

The three year mean concentration of daily maximum 8-hour rolling averaged ozone for 2009-2011. (More information)

Annual_Tons_Km2

The annual tons of diesel emissions for 2014. (More information)

Average_RSEI_Concentrations

the toxicity-weighted concentrations of chemical releases to air from facility emissions and off-site incineration averaged over a three-year period (2012-2014). (More information)

Browse Cities

A list of all cities in Washington state with coordinates allowing the users to 'fly to' or focus screens on selected cities (More information)

Treatment:

In order to allow for the data from each dataset to be readily comparable across different scales, each variable used in the map was normalized using min-max normalization. The normalized values of each variable were then used to get the height and color of census tract polygons. In the map, when a census tract is clicked upon the actual pollutant value is shown after the census tract and is then followed by the census tract's normalized value.

Aside from min-max normalization, the geometry of the data was clipped using a cartographic boundary map from the United States census bureau. Clipping was performed on the data so as to limit census tract extents to only land area, thereby improving geographic legibility.
